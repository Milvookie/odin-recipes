Korean sweet and spicy seitan

Description

A vegan alternative to the famous korean fried chicken. Seitan is a meat substitute made by extracting gluten from flour, it's very easy -and cheap- to make and has been used in asian cuisine for over a millenia.

Ingredients

  • Flour (600g)
  • Cornstarch
  • Neutral oil
  • Gochujang (1tbsp)
  • Sesame oil (2tbsp)
  • Soy sauce (1tbsp)
  • Mirin -Rice vinegar- (1tbsp)
  • Honey (2tbsp)
  • Sesame seeds
  • Green onion
  • Rice
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic powder

Instructions

  1. Make the rice in a rice cooker.
  2. In a bowl, mix the flour with 1/3 water (3 volume flour for 1 volume water). The dough needs to be elastic.
  3. Knead the dough to allow the gluten to form. The longer the kneading the better.
  4. Rinse the dough with cold water until only the gluten is left (the gluten -or seitan- will be a slightly darker color than the dough)
  5. In a bowl, mix the cornstarch, garlic powder, salt and pepper. Cut the Seitan in bite size pieces and toss them in the cornstarch mix.
  6. Heat neutral oil in a pan. When hot, add the seitan and fried until golden. Remove and set aside.
  7. In the pan on medium heat, add Gochujang, sesame oil, soy sauce, mirin and honey. Bring to a simmer.
  8. When the sauce began to thicken, add the seitan, mix to coat it.
  9. Serve on rice, add sesame seeds and green onions for toppings. Enjoy!
